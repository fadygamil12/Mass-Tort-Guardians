The Roundup lawsuit pertains to legal actions against the manufacturer of the herbicide Roundup, which contains the active ingredient glyphosate. Glyphosate has been the subject of controversy and litigation due to allegations that exposure to the herbicide is linked to an increased risk of cancer, particularly non-Hodgkin's lymphoma. Monsanto, the original manufacturer of Roundup, faced numerous lawsuits claiming that the company failed to adequately warn consumers about the potential health risks associated with glyphosate exposure. In 2018, Bayer AG acquired Monsanto and inherited the Roundup-related litigation. Several lawsuits have resulted in significant verdicts against Bayer, with juries finding the company liable for individuals developing cancer after using Roundup. The litigation has brought attention to the safety of glyphosate-containing herbicides and has led to discussions about regulatory standards for such products.
